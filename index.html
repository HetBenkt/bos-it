Antal Bos

Sr. Technical Consultant

Specialized in OpenText AppWorks
Java enthusiast
Simplified trainer

Building low-code solutions

OpenText AppWorks is key in building a solution on a low-code platform. AppWorks uses entity modelling for quick prototyping and BPMs for configuring advanced features. After my first contact I became a big proponent and through my blog appworks-tips.com I'm sharing my weekly knowledge which brought me to the expert I'm now.

Giving advice on best practices

With my 15 years of ECM experience in the field I learned from a lot of lessons. The failed activities brought me the expertise I have currently. This greatly helps in giving a solid advise on best practices for the AppWorks platform. Created solution can always be improved based on my own experience. This can also improve your team on structuring a solution.

Steering the customer

A customer always has a set of requirements which need to be build into a solution. My task as a consultant is to make sure those requirements fit into the platform. To make a steady future for the platform it's important to meet the potential of the product and don't pass its limits.

Building integrations

Every customer has its own systems in place to handle specific tasks. AppWorks has an enriched set of connectors available to communicate with these external type of systems. Systems like CMS, SAP, or AD. I brought alive several connectors already since day one of my own AppWorks journey.

Maintenance & troubleshooting

A solution running on a production environment requires maintenance and troubleshooting. Not only on solution level (functional), but also on service level (technical). Always keep focus on a steady environment where easy upgrades are key for a bright platform future. I learned to use several tools on my projects for investigating problems and do troubleshooting tasks.

Migration & data conversion

Every customer has its legacy systems with stored data. This data is most of the time key for the business to continue working. The AppWorks platform has several input streams to read this data and do conversion on it. My end-goal for your valuable data would be to migrate it into the new platform.

Monitoring & reporting

AppWorks is an ongoing platform with services, packages, data generation, and collaboration. All these activities require monitoring through logfile analyses and reporting. My experience can help to build those reports for your managers and put monitoring in place for your administrator.

Installation & configuration

Installing the AppWorks software became my sixth sense. During the last couple of years I installed every version to review the latest released functionality. During this exploration I saw a lot of configuration setting passing by. Your installation will not be that different where even a silent installation is possible to do.

Providing training

Keeping myself up to date and helping others on forums is great, but the most value delivery happens through a training session. Questions can be asked directly where we can even have a discussion around a specific topic. Training does not only help your team, but also my own experience will grow from the perspective of other people.

HTML5 customization

When client side components require an extension this is done via HTML5. My self-taught learning skills make it possible to quickly dive into a new JS technology to make the implementation happen. This can be a standalone application talking to AppWorks or an integrated page communicating with AppWorks.

Java modules & extensions

When a server side platform component requires an extension this is done via Java. I already wrote several modules for other projects and know my way in IntelliJ with Maven build tools and writing Unit tests. The SOLID principles of programming are familiar, and I like to read a book about Java to improve my skills.
